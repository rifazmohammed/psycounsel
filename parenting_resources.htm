General Parenting Guidelines

Be a Model Parent

 Children often learn by observing their parents. For example, if parents handle frustrations well, their children will probably learn to handle their own frustrations well.

Praise your Child

 Parents should praise children for good behaviour. Do not wait to praise till they do something exceptional. Catch them being good!

Give Affection

 Give affection to your children. Hug and give "love pats". Such acts should be frequent. Parents can send a message by not providing this affection soon after their children misbehave.

Discipline

Corrections should be immediate and administered in a matter-of-fact manner. Parents should not become upset while disciplining their children. Good old "Time-outs" and "Grounding" techniques still work if used correctly. However, after punishment children should be allowed to start fresh and should not be reminded of their past wrongs.

Consistency Pays

Parents should clearly state what they consider as acceptable behaviors. They should also be consistent in their expectations. Children's behavior should be handled in a similar manner by both parents. Consistency between parents and behavioral expectations on a day-to-day basis is essential. Consistency in the use of discipline is also very essential.

Give Specific Directions

Always make eye contact, before giving a direction to a child. Do not yell directions from elsewhere. Directions should be specific and concise. Vague directions such as "Be good" should be avoided. Your idea of being good and theirs may be totally different. Praise should be given to the children when they follow directions. It is necessary for the parent to ensure that directions are followed. If required they must be enforced so that the children learn that their parents mean what they say.

Have Clear Rules

Make rules clear and specific. Rules should be enforceable and enforcement matter-of-fact. The penalty for breaking rules must be known and any breaking of rules must be appropriately punished.

Allow Children to Help

Let your children help with household tasks. They enjoy this time spent with their parents.

Monitor Children

Parents should make sure they know where their children are and what they are doing. Rights to privacy in any form can be disregarded when parents suspect any form of danger the child may be in and need adult intervention.

Avoid Loud Verbal Disciplining

Lecturing, nagging, yelling, and screaming are generally not effective disciplining methods.
