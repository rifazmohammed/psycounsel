From a student's point of view, all that effort at education is to achieve a career that excites you and gives you a decent livelihood. There are, what we call the seven mantras or qualities that are essential for success. Let us take a cursory look at these.

1. Communication Skills.

Majority of the activity that goes on in any organisation is "communication". You will realize this if you take a critical look around you. What is not communication is probably dependent on something to initiate communication or keep it going. It is obvious that one needs to master the art of communication both verbal as well as written.

2. Influencing Abilities.

It includes persuading and negotiating skills and is considered as a leadership trait in management jargon. Persuasion involves convincing and encouraging people to do things, compared to being pushy and aggressive. This ability will stand you in good stead particularly in conflicting situations, in your professional as well as personal life.

3. Problem Solving Abilities.

It involves the ability to recognize and define problems, implement solutions and track and evaluate results. Effective problem solving skills will help you to become the corner stone of any team, which in turn will help your career prospects.

4. Creative Thinking.

It is said that intelligence is central to both the right and left sides of the brain. Generally, we tend to give more importance to the logical approach in problem solving. What is required is a combination of logical and lateral thinking processes, to generate new ideas and solutions.

5. Management Skills.

In a layman's words, this means "getting things done". From your career perspective, it involves managing things, people and yourself, viz. your time, resources and money. To be able to achieve success one needs to prepare and train oneself in the art of effective management.

6. Financial Management.

One should be adept at figures, statistics and accounts and be shrewd in money matters. As an old saying goes, " Money begets Money".

7. Social Skills.

It points to one's ability to relate to people, understanding them, helping others and facilitating. Interdependence is of greater value than independence in the present scenario. You, therefore, are required to take a genuine interest in people with whom you work and spent time with.
